###### Abstract

The recent discovery of the topologically protected surface states in
the β phase of PdBi₂ has reignited the research interest in this class
of superconductors. Here, we show results of our muon spin relaxation
and rotation (μSR) measurements carried out to investigate the
superconducting and magnetic properties and the topological effect in
the superconducting ground state of β-PdBi₂. Zero-field μSR data reveal
that no sizable spontaneous magnetization arises with the onset of
superconductivity implying that the time reversal symmetry is preserved
in the superconducting state of β-PdBi₂. Further, a strong diamagnetic
shift of the applied field has been observed in the transverse-field
(TF) μSR experiments, indicating that any triplet-pairing channel, if
present, does not dominate the superconducting condensate. Using TF-μSR,
we estimate that the magnetic penetration depth λ = 263(10) nm at zero
temperature. The nature of λ(T) provides evidence for the existence of a
nodeless single s-wave type isotropic energy gap of 0.78(1) meV at zero
temperature. Our results further suggest that the topologically
protected surface states have no effect on the bulk of the
superconductor.

where A(0) is the initial asymmetry of the sample signal, A_(bg) is the

TF-μSR measurements were carried out to derive the microscopic
properties of the superconducting state in β-PdBi₂. Figures 3(a) and
3(b) display the TF-μSR time spectra, collected at 7 K and 20 mK under a
magnetic field of 30 mT. The μSR time spectra obtained in the normal
state show a much weaker relaxation compared to the one in the
superconducting state. This is because the local field probed by the
muons in the normal state is produced essentially by the applied field
and the signal is only slightly damped due to the presence of nuclear
moments in the sample. A more pronounced damping is observed in the
superconducting state, caused by the inhomogeneous field distribution
generated by the formation of a vortex lattice. The TF-μSR time spectra
were analyzed using the following oscillatory decaying Gaussian
function:

Figure 4(a) reveals the temperature dependence of σ of β-PdBi₂ for an
applied field of 30 mT, which exhibits a pronounced change at
$T=T_{\rm c}$. The temperature dependence of the internal magnetic field
at the muon site with the expected diamagnetic shift below $T_{\rm c}$

The superconducting contribution σ_(sc) to σ can be derived by
quadratically subtracting the nuclear moment contribution
$\sigma_{\rm nm}$ (measured above $T_{\rm c}$) as
${\sigma_{sc}}^{2}=\sigma^{2}-{\sigma_{\rm nm}}^{2}$. $\sigma_{\rm nm}$
is assumed to be constant in the temperature range considered here. In
an isotropic type-II superconductor with a hexagonal Abrikosov vortex
lattice described by Ginzburg-Landau theory, the magnetic penetration
depth λ is related to σ_(sc) by the Brandt equation [28]

$$\sigma_{\rm sc}\ [\mu s^{-1}] = 4.854\times 10^{4}\bigg{(}1-\frac{H}{H_{\rm c2}}\bigg{)}$$
(3)
$$\times\bigg{[}1+1.21\bigg{(}1-\sqrt{\frac{H}{H_{\rm c2}}}\bigg{)} ^{3}\bigg{]}\lambda^{-2}\ [{\rm nm}^{-2}],$$

where H and $H_{\rm c2}$ are the applied and upper critical field,
respectively. The temperature dependence of λ⁻²(T) was calculated using
Eq. (3) where $H_{\rm c2}(T)$ was obtained from Ref. [6] by averaging
over the values collected parallel to ab and c axis. Figure 5 shows
λ⁻²(T) which is proportional to the effective superfluid density,
ρ_(s) ∝ λ⁻². This provides a possibility to study the gap symmetry in
the electronic density of states in the proximity of the Fermi energy
below $T_{\rm c}$ in β-PdBi₂. Figure 5 reveals that the ρ_(s) is nearly
constant below $T_{\rm c}/3\approx 2$ K, suggesting a nodeless
superconducting gap in β-PdBi₂. A reasonable fit to λ⁻²(T) can be
achieved with a single-gap BCS s-wave model (solid blue line in Fig. 5)
[29, 30]:

$$\frac{\lambda^{-2}(T)}{\lambda^{-2}(0)}=1+2\int_{\Delta(T)}^{\infty}\bigg{(} \frac{\partial f}{\partial E}\bigg{)}\frac{EdE}{\sqrt{E^{2}-\Delta(T)^{2}}}.$$
(4)

Here, λ⁻²(0) is the zero-temperature value of the magnetic penetration
depth and f = [1 + exp (E/k_(B)T)]⁻¹ denotes the Fermi function. The BCS
temperature dependence of the superconducting gap function is
approximated as [31]

$$\Delta(T)=\Delta(0)\tanh{\bigg{\{}1.82\bigg{[}1.018\bigg{(}\frac{T_{\rm c}}{T }-1\bigg{)}\bigg{]}^{0.51}\bigg{\}}},$$
(5)

where Δ(0) is the gap magnitude at zero temperature. From the fit, we
obtain $T_{\rm c}=4.84(1)$ K, λ(0) = 263(10) nm, and Δ(0) = 0.78(1) meV.
The gap to $T_{\rm c}$ ratio $\Delta(0)/k_{\rm B}T_{\rm c}=1.87(3)$ is
slightly higher than the BCS value of 1.76. Our results are consistent
with the reported values on β-PdBi₂, measured using STM by Herrera _et
al._[7].

To put our finding on β-PdBi₂ in the context of other superconductors,
we plot the Uemura graph of $T_{\rm c}$ versus effective Fermi
temperature $T_{\rm F}$ (see Fig. 6), which is often used to define the
character of unconventionality of a superconductor [32, 33]. $T_{\rm F}$
for β-PdBi₂ was calculated using the 3D expression as discussed in Ref.
[34]. We estimate $T_{\rm F}=6497$ K for β-PdBi₂, which places this
material well outside the broad gray line for unconventional
superconductors, such as cuprates and iron-based superconductors.
Conventional elemental superconductors are also generally considered to

In conclusion, μSR studies have been performed on the superconducting
β-PdBi₂. ZF-μSR data show no sign of a magnetic anomaly in the
superconducting ground state of β-PdBi₂. TF-μSR measurements were
performed to study the temperature dependence of the London penetration
depth λ which is proportional to the superfluid density ρ. λ(T) is very
well modeled within a single-gap BCS s-wave scenario with Δ(0) = 0.78(1)
meV, provide well-founded evidence for nodeless superconductivity in
β-PdBi₂. The magnetic penetration depth was estimated as λ(0) = 263(10)
nm. The value of λ⁻²(0) places β-PdBi₂ well outside the broad line for
unconventional superconductors in a Uemura plot. Our results further
suggest that the topologically protected surface states have very little
or no effect on the bulk of the superconductor. It is important to
mention here that in the bulk-μSR technique muons penetrate typically a
few hundreds of μm with a range straggling of the order of ∼100 μm. By
contrast, we expect the spatial extension of the surface Andreev bound
states to be of the order of the coherence length, i.e., a few tens of
nanometer. Further studies using surface probes are therefore required
to understand the evolution of the superconductivity at the surface. By
the time we submitted our manuscript, we noticed a new article on this
material in arXiv.org by J. Kacmarcik _et al._[36] in which they report
calorimetric, Hall-probe magnetometry and STM measurements. They also
found standard single s-wave gap superconductivity, consistent with our
finding.

The μSR experiments were performed at the Swiss Muon Source (SμS), Paul
Scherrer Institute (PSI), Villigen, Switzerland.
